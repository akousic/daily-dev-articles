## Summary

Our system architectures have changed as technology and development practices have evolved, but the way we practice architecture hasn’t kept up. According to Andrew Harmel-Law, architecture needs to be decentralized, similar to how we have decentralized our systems. At Goto Copenhagen, he presented the architecture advice process.

## Key Takeaways

- The biggest challenge in architecture is to be in all the right places at all the right times, Harmel-Law said.
- Architectural decisions are happening all over the place, and all the time.
- If there are only a named few who are responsible and accountable for all decisions, they need to be taking the best decisions for everyone based on all the right information and context.
